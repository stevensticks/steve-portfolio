Chapter 3 - 1965

Performance Art

Four songs. That’s right, four. Four out of twelve songs on Here Are The Sonics are original. The rest are the band performing other songs.(1) That shit would not fly now. Not for a second.(2) Especially because some of these songs were not only performed by other people but they were huge hits by other people. No record label in the world would release a record in this format currently.

But here we are 50 years later and I’ve chosen this record above all other records from 1965 to write about. Why? Because it is a hugely influential record and I’d say it’s a more important music event then Dylan’s decision to go electric on his ’65 album Highway 61 Revisited. Why? Because the fellas from The Sonics knew that they didn’t need to be brimming with talent to carve out a name in the music landscape. They needed attitude and energy. Which they brought in by the truckload to each one of these twelve songs. These songs sweat it. They took eight songs written by other people and owned them. The out performed their predecessors and made these songs The Sonics songs and while doing that, paved the way for bands like The Clash, Ramones and Sex Pistols to come along.

1/ This is pretty typical of the 60s especially the early 60s. On last weeks Pain In My Heart less then half of the album was originals.

2/ I know a lot of pop singers these days don’t write their own their own music but their music is still presented as if they did write it.
